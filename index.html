Five entropy metrics

Htext — Content Entropy

H = −Σ pi log₂ pi

Shannon entropy of page text word frequencies. High-entropy pages have unpredictable, fragmented content.

R — Information Flow Rate

R = ΔH / Δt

Rate of new content emergence. Short video feeds have extremely high R; deep articles have near-zero R.

Hrate — Transition Entropy

H = −Σ πi Pij log₂ Pij

Markov chain entropy rate of your navigation sequence. Random jumping between sites = high entropy rate.

G — Goal Relevance

G ∈ [0, 1]

How related is the current page to your daily anchor? High G means even high-entropy pages are productive.

D — Distraction Index

D = Hrate × (1 − G) × f(switches)

The composite metric. Combines navigation randomness, goal irrelevance, and tab-switching frequency into a single real-time distraction score.

Your attention is a thermodynamic system. Focus is low entropy — ordered, predictable, goal-directed. Mind-wandering is high entropy — disordered, random, dissipative. Negentropy gives you the instruments to measure and manage this process.

Install

🌐 Google Chrome / Edge

Load unpacked (developer mode, 30 seconds)

⬇ Download .zip

Install steps:

  1. Download negentropy-v1.0.0.zip and unzip it
  2. Open chrome://extensions/ in Chrome
  3. Enable Developer mode (top right toggle)
  4. Click Load unpacked → select the unzipped folder
  5. Done! The ❄️ icon appears in your toolbar
